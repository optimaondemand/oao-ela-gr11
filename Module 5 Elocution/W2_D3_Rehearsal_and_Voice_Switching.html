Week 2, Day 3: Rehearsal and Voice Switching

Project: American Voices in Dialogue

Objective: Rehearse with intentional voice switching, physical shifts, and dynamic pacing.

Standards: ELA.11.C.2.1, ELA.K12.EE.6.1

💡 Key Concept: The hardest part of solo multi-voice performance is the transition. A clean switch requires a pause, a physical reset, a vocal reset, and immediate commitment.

📖 Lesson

Run your full 3-4 minute dialogue at least twice today, recording each run.

Use this transition checklist every time you switch voices:

  1. Pause: Take a full beat of silence.
  2. Reset your body: Shift posture, gaze, physical energy.
  3. Reset your voice: Change pace, pitch, or volume before the first word.
  4. Commit immediately: Hit the new voice from the first syllable.
✅ Pro Tip
What feels exaggerated to you as the performer usually sounds just right to a listener. Push the contrast further than feels comfortable.

🔬 Check Your Understanding

Both voices sound too similar. Best fix?

A. Announce speaker names before each switch
B. Exaggerate vocal contrast: one voice slower/deeper, the other quicker/lighter. Add longer pauses between switches.
C. Stand farther from camera for one voice
D. Switch to different texts
Increase contrast. Exaggerate pace, pitch, and volume differences, and take a full pause between switches.
