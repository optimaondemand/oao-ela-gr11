Week 1, Day 1: The Voice as an Instrument

Project: Revolutionary Voice

Objective: Understand diaphragmatic breathing and projection as foundational vocal techniques.

Standards: ELA.11.C.2.1, ELA.K12.EE.6.1

💡 Key Concept: Projection is not volume. It is breath support. When you control your diaphragm, your voice carries without strain.

📖 Lesson

Welcome to the Elocution Unit! Over the next three weeks, you will develop your skills as a speaker and performer through independent projects that build on each other.

Great speakers don't just say words; they deliver them. Projection isn't about yelling; it's about supporting your voice with breath from your diaphragm. When you breathe deeply (your belly expands, not your shoulders), you create a column of air that carries your voice without strain.

✅ Pro Tip
Try placing your hand on your belly while breathing. If your hand moves outward on the inhale, you're doing it right. If your shoulders rise instead, reset and try again.

🔬 Check Your Understanding

When practicing diaphragmatic breathing, which physical response indicates you're doing it correctly?

A. Your shoulders rise with each inhale
B. Your belly expands outward as you inhale
C. You feel tightness in your throat
D. Your chest puffs out dramatically
The diaphragm pushes downward, expanding the belly and filling lungs from the bottom. This creates steady, supported airflow.
