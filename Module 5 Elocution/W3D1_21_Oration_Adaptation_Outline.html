Oration Adaptation Outline

Week 3, Day 1 · ✍️ Written Response

Points: 20

🔗 Connection to Lesson: This outline bridges your written essay and your spoken oration.

📋 Assignment

Re-read your Module 4 argument essay. Create an adaptation outline:

1. OPENING HOOK: New spoken opening (2-3 sentences)
2. THESIS: Rewrite as a clear spoken claim
3. KEY POINTS: 2-3 strongest arguments; how to present each orally
4. CLOSING: New spoken ending

📤 How to Submit

Submit a document or type directly. All four sections should be clearly labeled.

🎯 What Your Teacher Is Looking For

  • Opening hook is engaging and distinct from the essay's introduction
  • Thesis is rewritten as a speakable claim
  • Key points include oral presentation strategy notes
  • Closing is designed to move a listener
⚠️ Heads Up
Read your response aloud before submitting. If it doesn't make sense spoken, revise it. Your teacher is looking for genuine thinking, not filler. Short and honest beats long and vague.
