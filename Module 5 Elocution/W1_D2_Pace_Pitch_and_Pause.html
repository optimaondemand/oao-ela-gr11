Week 1, Day 2: Pace, Pitch, and Pause

Project: Revolutionary Voice

Objective: Experiment with pacing, pitch variation, and strategic pauses to shape meaning.

Standards: ELA.11.C.2.1, ELA.K12.EE.6.1

💡 Key Concept: Pace, pitch, and pause are the three tools that separate reading aloud from performing. Each one shapes how a listener experiences your words.

📖 Lesson

Pace: Speaking quickly creates urgency. Slowing down signals importance and gives your listener time to absorb meaning.

Pitch: Varying your pitch keeps listeners engaged. A rising pitch signals a question; a falling pitch conveys authority and finality.

Pause: Silence is not emptiness. A well-placed pause before or after a key phrase gives it weight and lets your listener think.

Patrick Henry didn't rush through "Give me liberty, or give me death!" He built to it, paused, and let the room feel the stakes.

✅ Pro Tip
Record yourself reading a passage, then listen back. Most new speakers are surprised by how fast they go and how few pauses they take. Slow down more than feels natural; it will sound just right to a listener.

A. The speaker forgot the next line

Strategic pauses allow the listener to absorb the impact. This is one of the most effective, and underused, rhetorical tools.
