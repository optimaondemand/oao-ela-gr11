Week 1, Day 4: Self-Evaluation and Revision

Project: Revolutionary Voice

Objective: Record a practice performance, evaluate your delivery, and revise for your final recording.

Standards: ELA.K12.EE.4.1, ELA.11.C.2.1

💡 Key Concept: The difference between a good speaker and a great one is self-evaluation. Recording yourself and listening back honestly is how professionals improve.

📖 Lesson

Today you become your own coach. Record a full practice run of your annotated passage, then listen back and evaluate yourself on these criteria:

Projection & Clarity: Can you hear every word clearly?

Pace & Pause: Did you rush? Were pauses intentional?

Tone & Emotion: Does delivery match your annotations?

Emphasis: Are marked words actually standing out?

✅ Pro Tip
Listen to your recording with your eyes closed. If you can't tell where the emphasis and pauses are without seeing your annotations, they aren't strong enough yet.

🔬 Check Your Understanding

Your voice drops in volume and energy during the middle section. Most effective fix?

A. Speak louder throughout
B. Annotate the middle with specific energy cues: tone shifts, emphasis, projection notes
C. Cut the middle section
D. Speed through it
Targeted annotation fixes for specific sections work better than global volume changes.
