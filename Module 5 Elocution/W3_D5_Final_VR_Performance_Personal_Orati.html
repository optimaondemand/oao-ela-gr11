Week 3, Day 5: Final VR Performance: Personal Oration and Unit Reflection

Project: Personal Oration

Objective: Submit your polished 4-5 minute VR oration and reflect on your growth across the unit.

Standards: ELA.11.C.2.1, ELA.K12.EE.6.1, ELA.K12.EE.4.1, ELA.11.C.5.1

💡 Key Concept: You studied voices that shaped the American conversation. In Module 4 you researched how those arguments live today. Now you are doing what those voices did: standing up and making your case.

📖 Lesson

Final checklist before recording:

  • VR environment set with professional backdrop
  • Mnemonic aids and IFX cues in place
  • Props/multimedia positioned and ready
  • Opening hook, thesis, and closing internalized (no notes for these)
  • Vocal score reviewed
  • Dress as you would for a formal presentation

Trust your preparation, your argument, and your voice. Make this a recording you would be proud to show anyone.

✅ Pro Tip
Before you hit record: stand still for five seconds, breathe, and remind yourself what you believe about this issue. Then begin.

✍️ Written Response

Unit Reflection (answer ALL, aim for a full page):

1. Which of the three projects (Revolutionary Voice, Voices in Dialogue, Personal Oration) felt most authentic? Why?

2. What vocal technique had the biggest impact across the unit? Give a specific example from one of your recordings.

3. How was delivering your Module 4 argument as a spoken oration different from writing it as an essay? What did you discover by speaking it aloud?

4. How did self-recording and self-evaluating change your understanding of your speaking abilities? What surprised you?

5. What did you learn about elocution? How could you apply these skills to presentations, interviews, debates, or other speaking contexts?

A. Memorizing speeches and reciting without mistakes
B. Speaking as loudly and dramatically as possible
