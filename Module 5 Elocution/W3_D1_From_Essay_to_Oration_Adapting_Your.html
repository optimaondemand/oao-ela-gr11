Week 3, Day 1: From Essay to Oration: Adapting Your Argument

Project: Personal Oration

Objective: Begin adapting your Module 4 argument essay into a 4-5 minute oration for VR delivery.

Standards: ELA.11.C.2.1, ELA.11.C.1.5, ELA.K12.EE.6.1

💡 Key Concept: Writing for the ear is fundamentally different from writing for the page. A listener hears your argument once, in real time. Clarity, conviction, and hooks matter more than citations and hedging.

📖 Lesson

For your final project, you will transform your Module 4 research-based argument essay into a polished oration delivered in VR. This is not reading your essay aloud.

What stays: Your thesis, core argument, strongest evidence, and the connection between Revolutionary-era texts and your contemporary issue.

What changes: Long sentences become shorter. Block quotes become paraphrased highlights. Transitions become spoken signposts. Your intro needs a hook.

What gets added: Personal conviction. You wrote as a researcher; now deliver as an advocate.

✅ Pro Tip
Read your essay's introduction out loud. If it sounds like a paper, it is. Rewrite it as something you would actually say to a person you want to persuade.

🎯 Activities

📍 Checkpoint: Complete all activities below before moving to the next lesson.

🔬 Check Your Understanding

Your essay opens: "The Federalist Papers established a framework for understanding factions." Stronger spoken hook?

A. Keep as-is
B. "I will discuss factions and polarization."
C. "240 years ago, Madison warned factions would tear the republic apart. Open any news app today, and you'll see he was right."
D. "Factions are important."
A spoken hook needs immediate tension. Link the historical warning to everyday experience.

✍️ Written Response

Adaptation outline from your Module 4 essay:

1. OPENING HOOK: New spoken opening (2-3 sentences)
2. THESIS: Rewrite as a spoken claim you could say with conviction
3. KEY POINTS: 2-3 strongest arguments; note how you will present each orally
4. CLOSING: New spoken ending (call to action, resonant image, or memorable line)

✍️ Written Response

Performance Intent paragraph: What should your listener think, feel, or do afterward? How will your spoken version differ from the written essay? Where will your conviction be strongest?
